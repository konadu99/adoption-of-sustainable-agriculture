Barriers to Adoption

Despite its benefits, sustainable agriculture faces several challenges in Kentucky and beyond. These include:

  • High initial investment: Transitioning to new technologies, such as no-till planters or drip systems, requires upfront capital.
  • Limited knowledge and technical capacity: Some farmers lack access to extension training or demonstration plots (Reimer & Prokopy, 2019).
  • Short-term uncertainty: The benefits of sustainable practices often accumulate over several years, discouraging short-term adoption.
  • Policy and incentive limitations: Current conservation programs may not adequately support smaller farms transitioning to sustainable systems.

Extension services, demonstration farms, and peer learning are essential to overcome these barriers. Collaborative programs between universities, agencies, and farmers are gradually improving the adoption rate across Kentucky (UK Extension, 2024).
